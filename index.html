Example: Prospecting Trees Structure

(This was built specifically for Quoala's employee engagement product - we'd create something entirely different for Ladybug)

The structure of sales vertical targeting and customer account growth within Quoala. Each prospecting tree consists of One Level 1 "Strategic" target supported by 3 Level 3 "Expansion" targets, and 5 Level 5 "Foothold" targets. This structure applies to industry verticals, use cases, and customer account growth.

With no exception, sales personnel own all leads included in an assigned prospecting tree.

As a visual example, the coffee roasting vertical could have Starbucks as the Strategic Level 1 Target with key Expansion targets Pete's, Caribou, and The Coffee Bean. A Foothold in the coffee vertical would start with smaller Level 5 targets like Cafe Vita, Woods, Stumptown, Chocolati, and Intelligentsia Coffee.

Within a customer organization, prospecting trees represent account growth potential. In all cases, the strategic objective is "Company-Wide" adoption. Expansion and Foothold achievement will be measured and evaluated by individual sales teams.

Example: Strategic Sales Motions

(How this organization defined strategic sales motions in line with prospecting trees)

Directed Prospecting - actions taken to identify target sales that will assist in larger sales in the future. These actions include:

  • Identifying strategic company purchase strategy
  • Identifying optimal referral customers (People potential customers will listen to)
  • Identifying complementary or rival customers (Companies that other companies will follow)

Referred Evaluation - actions taken to trigger a customer evaluation from an existing customer or negotiating customer. This includes referral evaluation from customer industry contacts and additional evaluation executed within large existing customers.

Note: "A purchase oriented referral meeting is the gold standard for sales performance at Quoala."

Prospect Tree Revenue Growth - actions taken to increase the overall financial value of a specified prospect tree. In ideal circumstances, Foothold sales that trigger Expansion sales and eventually Strategic sales.

Referenceable Accounts - actions taken to publicly showcase successful customer launches, using reference customers to help demonstrate value to potential new customers.

What's important here is that these aren't generic sales activities - they're specifically designed to work within the prospecting tree framework. Each motion reinforces the others and builds systematic momentum.
